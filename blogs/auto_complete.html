Introducing User-Defined Ghost Autocomplete

When presenting code, explanations, or prepared text, it’s easy to momentarily forget the next word or sentence. Pauses, glances at notes, or mistyped lines can disrupt your flow and make your audience lose focus. Ghost autocomplete solves this by giving subtle, predictive hints for the next word in your predefined lines as you type. You stay on track, maintain a smooth rhythm, and keep your audience engaged — all without looking away from your editor.

How It Works

You can define any sentences or lines you plan to type during your presentation:

  • Code lines, comments, or explanations.
  • Long paragraphs of instructions.
  • Repeated phrases or complex expressions.

As you start typing, the editor predicts the next word and displays it as ghost text. Press Tab or keep typing to accept it. This allows you to type accurately and quickly, without breaking the audience's attention.

Example sentences used in the demo:
"This is a long sentence for testing autocomplete."
"When cookies are not designed properly, security issues appear."
And more...
