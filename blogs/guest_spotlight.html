The Struggle of the Large Lecture Hall

As educators, we’ve all been there: A student in the very back row raises their hand to offer a brilliant insight. They start speaking, but their voice is swallowed by the hum of the air conditioner or the sheer size of the hall. You have to ask them to repeat it—or worse—you have to "translate" and repeat their words for the rest of the class.

"When students can't be heard, they stop participating. Communication becomes a one-way street."

Introducing Guest Spotlight

Presentation Assistants is proud to debut a world-first feature designed specifically for large-scale interaction. Guest Spotlight allows an instructor to "beam" a student's voice directly to the room's sound system using the student's own smartphone or laptop as the microphone.

From Smartphone to Speakers

When you select a student to answer, they receive an instant prompt on their screen. With one tap, their device connects to your presentation stage. Their voice is broadcast through the main speakers, ensuring that the student in the front row and the student in the back row hear the contribution with perfect clarity.

Equity in Participation

Soft-spoken students often hesitate to participate because they know they won't be heard. By providing a digital "megaphone," Guest Spotlight levels the playing field. It transforms a passive lecture into a dynamic dialogue where every seat is the "best seat in the house."

Total Instructor Control

You remain the director of the classroom. You choose when to "Spotlight" a guest and when to mute them. Our beautiful host interface gives you a real-time visualizer so you can see the audio activity, ensuring the technology never gets in the way of the teaching.

Why It’s a Game Changer

  • No specialized hardware: Students use the devices they already have in their pockets.
  • Instant Connection: Built on high-speed WebRTC technology for zero-lag conversation.
  • Room-Filling Sound: Audio is routed through the host's system to the audience speakers.
  • Enhanced Engagement: Encourages students at the back to stay active and vocal.
  • Professional Visuals: A sleek "On Air" UI keeps the audience focused on the speaker.

Don't let your classroom's size dictate the quality of your discussion. With Guest Spotlight, the back row is finally part of the conversation.
