Why Generic Calendars Often Fail

Traditional calendars are designed for personal scheduling. Each person manages their own events, and sharing resources often requires external links, emails, or separate file systems. Viewers frequently have to leave the calendar just to access files, causing friction and frustration.

Introducing Our One-Way Calendar

With Presentation Assistants, this isn’t your generic calendar. Instead of isolated personal planners, our calendar serves as a shared knowledge hub. Organizers can attach files and folders directly to events; viewers can open and download resources from the event page without bouncing between emails or cloud drives.

This is ideal for classrooms and universities: professors publish lecture notes, slides, and assignments while students access them seamlessly in one place. Corporate teams, workshops, and project managers can also leverage this approach to streamline content delivery and access.

No More Lost Meeting Notes

How many times have you attended a meeting only to find yourself two months later frantically digging through email threads, cloud folders, or group chats trying to locate the meeting notes? With Presentation Assistants, that frustration disappears. Meeting notes, slides, recordings, and related documents can be directly attached to the calendar event. When you look back at that meeting, everything you need is right there — no more searching across multiple tools, no more wasted time.

By keeping context and content together, Presentation Assistants ensures your institutional knowledge is preserved and instantly retrievable. Whether you’re a professor, project manager, or event organizer, attaching materials to the event solves the “where are the notes?” problem once and for all.
