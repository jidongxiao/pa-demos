A First-of-Its-Kind Terminal Experience

Live coding demos and command-line walkthroughs are notoriously difficult to share. Screen sharing often blurs text, lags behind, or becomes unreadable on mobile devices. Meanwhile, interactive terminals typically require complex setup steps, SSH keys, local installations, or give too much control to the audience.

Presentation Assistants introduces something entirely new: Live Synchronized Web Terminals. A presenter opens terminal tabs in their browser — and every audience member instantly gets a live terminal view that mirrors commands and output character-by-character, in real time.

  • Multiple tabs: Presenter can open as many terminal tabs as needed.
  • Real-time broadcast: All terminals are mirrored instantly to every audience member.
  • Presenter control: Only the presenter can type or execute commands.
  • View-only audience: Perfect for classes, demos, workshops, and conferences.
  • No installation: Everything runs directly in the browser.

Audience Perspective

From the audience’s point of view, it feels like watching a perfect, crisp, lag-free terminal — but it’s not a screen share. It’s their own browser rendering the terminal output as structured data.

  • Crystal-clear text.
  • No blurry compression or UI resizing issues.
  • Follow along easily, even on phones or tablets.
  • Zero risk, since they cannot execute commands.

Presenter Perspective

As the presenter, you stay fully in control:

  • Type commands normally — your audience instantly sees them.
  • Switch between multiple terminals during your demo.
  • No need to zoom text for readability.
  • No risk of audience interfering with the environment.
  • No installations, SSH configs, or environment issues.
