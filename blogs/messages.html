Voice Assistant Trigger Messages

Let your assistant respond instantly during live presentations.
Speak naturally, and the assistant will provide predefined responses when it detects your keywords.

Why Presenters Need This Feature

During live talks, lectures, or workshops, presenters often juggle multiple things at once: explaining concepts, writing code, navigating slides, and keeping an eye on the audience. It's easy to forget key details or miss points you intended to emphasize.

Our Voice Assistant Trigger Messages feature solves this by letting you define keyword-based responses in advance. During your presentation, simply mention the trigger keywords in your speech, and the assistant will immediately speak the pre-defined response aloud.

Example: Set your keywords to garbage and collector, with the response: “A garbage collector automatically frees unused memory so programs don’t leak resources.” When you ask, “What will the garbage collector do”? The assistant will read the explanation aloud.

How It Works

  • Predefine triggers: Choose two keywords for each message and write the response text.
  • Live speech recognition: The assistant listens to your words in real time.
  • Automatic responses: When both keywords appear in your speech, the assistant speaks the corresponding response immediately.
  • Hands-free: You can focus entirely on presenting without interacting with the device.
  • Maintain flow: Your audience gets additional context or explanations without interruption.

Why It Matters

This feature is perfect for:

  • Lecturers who want to give definitions, clarifications, or examples on the fly.
  • Conference speakers who need to deliver consistent explanations across sessions.
  • Workshops where supporting details should appear without breaking your teaching flow.
  • Presenters who want to reduce cognitive load and avoid looking at notes.
Think of it as a “smart, reactive assistant” that follows your speech and provides timely information, helping you maintain a polished and professional presentation.
